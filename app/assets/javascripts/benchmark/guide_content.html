HOW TO USE THE 2030 ENERGY TARGET & RATING TOOL

  1. Enter the Geographical Information about your Building (e.g. location)
  2. Enter the number of Heating Degree Days (HDD) and Cooling Degree Days (CDD) for your location. If you don’t know these, click on the for help.
  3. Select if your building is a new or existing building.
    1. Note: it is important that your zip code is correct for computing the correct carbon emissions
  4. Select the Primary Function of your Building
    1. For Architecture 2030, new constructions have a goal of performing 70% better the National Median
    2. Existing buildings have a goal of performing 20% better than the National Median Note: based on CBECS 2003 data
  5. Select the Primary Function of your Building
    1. This will expose additional fields for your specific building type
    2. You must fill each field – if you don’t know the value you can use the default values for your building type
    3. You can add any number of building uses
  6. Enter your annual energy use information if you want to know how your building is currently performing relative to the National Median
  7. Click ‘Compute Target’ to learn how close you are to your goal!
    1. The Baseline Column of the results table shows the metrics for the National Median of buildings similar to your own
    2. The Target Column shows the goal that you should be aiming for.
    3. The Rating Column shows how well your building is currently performing.
    4. If your Rating zEPI score is lower than the Target zEPI score, you are doing great!

To Learn more about the zEPI Score: http://newbuildings.org/code_policy/zepi/
