Municipal Portfolio Codes Assessment Use Case

Cities across the country are beginning to explore ways to improve the energy efficiency of their building portfolios. The process of identifying specific improvement strategies can be long and confusing, spanning multiple levels of analysis. The Portfolio Codes Assessment tool was designed to be the first step, by enabling municipalities to conduct a quick, simple analysis to understand the typical breakdown of energy end-uses for their buildings and the impact that different tiers of investment strategies may have on their overall energy-use and efficiency.

How was the Portfolio Codes Tool created?

The MPP team worked with the cities of Providence (RI), Grand Rapids (MI), and Eugene (OR) to develop and refine a process for Municipal Strategic Energy Management. Data for buildings across these portfolios, each located in a distinct climate zone, was used to calibrate precise energy models for the most common building types found in a municipal portfolio.

    Energy-use and building data was collected for 3 Municipal Portfolios in different climate zones
    Energy models were constructed and calibrated with actual energy use data
    Building energy end-use data was exported for each energy model
